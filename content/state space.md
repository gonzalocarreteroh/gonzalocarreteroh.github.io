 A state space is the set of all possible states a given system may be at a particular moment in time.

> [!EXAMPLE]-
> For a die of six sides:
> $\large S = \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6\}$
> 
>For the weather (we could consider more states):
> $\large S = \{sunny, rainy, snowy\}$

We may have **discrete** (e.g. $\large \mathbb{N}$) or **continuos** (e.g. $\large \mathbb{R}$) state spaces. They may also be **finite** or **infinite**.

More on this topic later, as we can say many things about it in the domain of control theory or physics.
